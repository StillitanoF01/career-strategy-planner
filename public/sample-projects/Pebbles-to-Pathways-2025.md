## Project description
**Statement:** A school and education precinct proposal at Sydney Olympic Park.
**Brief:** The brief called for an adaptive reuse strategy, reworking existing structures on site into a new education precinct rather than building from scratch.
**Design intent:** Cross-laminated timber (CLT) construction was the central design strategy, used as both a structural and material driver for adapting the existing buildings.
**Key challenges:** Developing the construction package — resolving how the CLT systems integrated with the retained existing structure in enough technical detail to be credible.
**Final outcome:** An urban plan for the precinct, supported by a CLT-based adaptive reuse strategy for the school buildings.

## Reflection
**What worked well:** The CLT construction strategy held up well as a design driver — it gave the adaptive reuse approach a clear technical and material logic.
**What could be improved:** Entourage and contextual representation in the renders could have been stronger.
**Lessons learned:** Working through an adaptive reuse brief reinforced how much the construction and material strategy (in this case CLT) can drive the rest of the design, from urban plan down to detail.
